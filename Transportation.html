Transportation

Bicycles

Cycling is a perfect way for families to explore Amsterdam. Renting a cargo bike allows parents to transport multiple children safely, with plenty of space for bags. The city’s extensive bike paths make riding safer and more enjoyable, keeping you away from busy streets.

Family-friendly rental shops offer child seats, helmets, and accessories, ensuring everyone is comfortable. Planning routes ahead and opting for scenic, quiet paths adds to the experience, with parks providing ideal stops for breaks.

Cycling through Amsterdam’s charming streets and canals creates unforgettable memories, offering a fun and active way to experience the city as a family.

Metro

Amsterdam's metro, though smaller than the tram and bus networks, is a convenient way to reach areas beyond the city center.

Family-Friendly Features: Most metro stations have elevators for easy stroller access, but parents should closely supervise children on platforms.

Tips for Families: Check for family tickets with child discounts, and consider walking or biking for shorter trips to enjoy the city’s charm.

The metro offers a practical option for families exploring Amsterdam's outskirts while staying connected to the city.

Buses

Buses complement Amsterdam's tram system, offering access to areas trams don't reach.

Family-Friendly Features: Most buses have low floors for easy stroller access and designated spaces. They also provide direct routes to attractions and neighborhoods, making them convenient for families.

Tips for Families: Buses use the same tickets as trams—consider a day pass for multiple trips. Use transport apps to plan routes and check schedules for smoother travel.

Buses offer a practical and accessible option for family outings across the city.
